## Notes & Observations

### Key Insights

1. **No-code/automation creators** are the best fit - they already teach audiences to build AI tools
2. **Data professionals** (engineers, scientists, CTOs) are decision-makers with budgets
3. **Technical educators** have highly engaged audiences who take action
4. **SaaS founders** can become case studies and advocates
5. **Cross-platform creators** provide more touchpoints for conversion

### Challenges Identified

1. **Email availability** - Most creators don't publicly list business emails
2. **Response rates** - Micro creators may have DMs/emails flooded
3. **Verification needed** - Some follower counts estimated from sources
4. **Contact timing** - Need to verify recent activity before outreach

### Success Factors

For highest conversion rates:
- Lead with **value** (free access, early features) not just commission
- Emphasize **alignment** with their content (AI agents, automation, APIs)
- Provide **ready-to-use content** (demo videos, graphics, talking points)
- Offer **exclusive access** (beta features, community, support)
- Create **co-marketing opportunities** (case studies, interviews, webinars)

## Appendix: Glossary

- **Nano Influencer:** 1K-10K followers
- **Micro Influencer:** 10K-100K followers
- **Engagement Rate:** Likes, comments, shares divided by followers
- **Affiliate Commission:** Percentage of revenue shared with creator
- **Qualified Sign-up:** User who completes registration and connects payment
- **Recurring Commission:** Commission paid monthly on subscription renewals
