### **Michael Porter (Competitive Strategy)**

I fundamentally disagree with a flat per-signup commission model. Look at your value chain: the creator's real value isn't driving signupsâ€”it's driving **quality users who transact**.

**My recommendation: Tiered revenue share**
- **15% of first-month subscription revenue** per referred user
- **5% ongoing for 12 months** (long-tail incentive)
- **Bonus tier**: 20% if they drive 10+ paying users in Month 1

**Rationale**:
- You're building a marketplace. Your CAC only matters if users transact (run agent collaborations).
- A $50/signup commission blindly rewards volume. A 15% rev share rewards *quality*.
- Competitive positioning: Most SaaS affiliates offer 20-30% one-time. You're offering recurring incomeâ€”that's differentiation.

**The trap**: Per-signup commissions attract affiliate spammers. You'll get 100 signups from bot traffic and zero transactions. Revenue share self-selects creators who understand product-market fit.

### **Nassim Nicholas Taleb (Risk & Uncertainty)**

Porter's revenue share is **fragile to uncertainty**. You have no idea:
- What your actual conversion rate is (signup â†’ paid)
- What your churn is
- If creators will even drive ONE paying user

**My recommendation: Asymmetric optionality**
- **$25 per qualified signup** (completed onboarding + created 1 agent)
- **$100 bonus per first transaction** (agent-to-agent payment executed)
- **Black swan upside**: If any creator drives 50+ qualified signups in Month 1, give them **equity** (0.1-0.25%)

**Rationale**:
- You're building antifragility. Small fixed payouts protect your downside (cash flow).
- The $100 transaction bonus creates skin in the gameâ€”they can't just spam signups.
- Equity for breakout performers creates convexity. If one creator drives 500 users, you want them locked in long-term, not jumping to a competitor.

**Porter's mistake**: Revenue share assumes you *have* revenue. You're pre-PMF. You need volume first, quality second. Fixed payments give you data without bleeding cash.

### **Clayton Christensen (Disruption Theory)**

You're both solving the wrong problem. The question isn't "$/signup vs % revenue"â€”it's **what job is the creator hiring your partnership to do?**

**Jobs-to-be-Done for micro-creators**:

**My recommendation: Hybrid with clarity**

**Rationale**:

- Non-consumption: Micro-creators *don't monetize* because rev-share is too uncertain. Fixed + variable makes the decision easy.

**Taleb's mistake**: Equity for micro-creators is noise. They don't understand it, won't value it, and it complicates legal. Keep it simple.

**Porter's mistake**: Pure rev-share is too abstract. "15% of first-month" requires them to trust your pricing, conversion rates, AND payout system. Too many unknowns = no action.
